EU Heating Oil — Purchasing Power Adjusted

With-tax price adjusted for purchasing power parity (PPP) · shows true affordability · source: EU Oil Bulletin + Eurostat PPP indices 2025

PPP adjustment accounts for different cost-of-living levels across EU countries. A country may have a low nominal price but high PPP-adjusted price if wages and purchasing power are lower. Ireland's high nominal price is partially offset by above-average purchasing power.

Gross Margin & Price Pass-Through

How crude cost changes translate to retail prices · EU Bulletin pre-tax + EIA Brent + ECB EUR/USD · 2-year weekly

Gross margin = Ireland pre-tax retail price (EU Oil Bulletin depot price, ex all tax) minus Brent crude cost converted to €/litre — it captures refinery processing, transport, distribution, and dealer markup combined. The ±1σ confidence bands show normal margin variability; excursions signal unusual pricing. Pass-through (β) is the fraction of a €1 move in Brent crude that appears in the pre-tax retail price. The model tests lags 0–3 weeks and picks the best-fit lag, detecting whether retailers respond immediately or with delay. β = 1.0 means full pass-through. β < 1.0 means costs are partially absorbed. If β_rising > β_falling, retailers transmit cost increases faster than cost savings — the standard empirical signal of asymmetric pricing and potential market power. Rolling asymmetry tracks how this behaviour evolves over 26-week sliding windows. Seasonal analysis compares heating season (Oct–Mar) vs off-season margins to detect winter demand premiums. Note: EU Bulletin prices are survey depot prices; oilprices.ie tracks the higher final delivery price.
